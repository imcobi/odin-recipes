How to Cook Salmon

This salmon dish features a perfectly seared fillet with a crisp golden crust on the outside and tender, flaky flesh inside.
The natural richness of the salmon is enhanced with a light seasoning of sea salt, cracked black pepper, and a squeeze of
fresh lemon juice, bringing out its delicate flavor. Served over a bed of roasted vegetables, the dish balances heartiness
with freshness, creating a vibrant and nourishing meal. A drizzle of herb-infused butter sauce ties everything together,
a fragrant layer of garlic, parsley, and dill that complements the salmon’s savory profile. The contrast of textures—crispy
skin, moist fish, and caramelized vegetables—makes each bite satisfying. Elegant yet simple, this salmon dish can stand as
a wholesome weeknight dinner or a centerpiece for a more refined occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb salmon fillet, skin on
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh herbs (such as dill or parsley) for garnish
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon butter for extra richness

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pat the salmon fillet dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, place the salmon fillet skin-side down in the skillet.
  4. Cook for about 4-5 minutes without moving it, allowing the skin to crisp up.
  5. Carefully flip the salmon fillet and cook for another 2 minutes on the other side.
  6. Add minced garlic and lemon slices around the salmon in the skillet.
  7. If using, add a tablespoon of butter to the skillet, letting it melt and baste the salmon.
  8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it rest for a couple of minutes. Garnish with fresh herbs before serving.
